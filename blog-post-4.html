Fact vs. Fiction:
5 Water Myths Debunked

Nov 02, 2025 Vishal Kumar Education

We all know we need to drink water to survive, but there is a lot of misinformation floating around about how, when, and what we should drink. At Viv-Elion, we believe informed customers make healthier choices.

Let's clear up the confusion and look at the science behind hydration.

Myth #1: You Must Drink 8 Glasses a Day

The Truth: This is an old guideline. Your water needs depend on your body weight, activity level, and the climate (especially in Punjab summers!). A better rule of thumb? Drink when you are thirsty, and check the color of your urine—it should be pale yellow.

Myth #2: All Water Tastes the Same

The Truth: Absolutely false. The taste of water is determined by its **TDS (Total Dissolved Solids)** and mineral content. Tap water often tastes like chlorine. Viv-Elion mineral water has a sweet, crisp taste because of the balanced Calcium and Magnesium.

Myth #3: Thirst is the First Sign of Dehydration

The Truth: By the time you actually feel thirsty, your body is already slightly dehydrated. Other early signs include fatigue, mild headache, or difficulty concentrating. Keep a bottle of Viv-Elion nearby to sip throughout the day.

"Water is the cheapest and most effective medicine for fatigue."

Myth #4: Plastic Bottles are Always Unsafe

The Truth: Not all plastic is created equal. At Viv-Elion, we use high-grade, food-safe PET material that does not leach chemicals into the water, provided it is stored away from direct sunlight. We also encourage recycling every bottle.

Conclusion

Don't rely on old myths. Listen to your body and choose a water source that is verified, pure, and rich in essential minerals.
